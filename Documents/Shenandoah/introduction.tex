\section{INTRODUCTION}

This is a short scenario for Columbia’s block game \textit{Shenandoah}. This covers the climactic period of the 1862 Valley Campaign, from Stonewall Jackson’s daring attack on Front Royal to the pivotal battles of Cross Keys and Port Republic.

\subsection{Scenario Length}

The scenario starts on turn 7 and ends at the conclusion of turn 12 (six turns). At the end of turn 12, the Confederate player checks for victory.

\subsection{How to Win}

In this scenario only the CSA player scores victory points. The CSA point total is equal to the sum of all controlled CSA and USA cities. Eliminated artillery, cavalry and infantry units are worth 1 VP, HQ’s are worth 2 VP and detachments are worth ½ VP for each. Eliminated USA units are added to the CSA score, while eliminated CSA units are subtracted from the CSA score.

At the end of the scenario, if the CSA player has scored at least 15 VP and controls either Winchester or Staunton, then the CSA player wins. Otherwise the USA player wins.

\subsubsection{Sudden Death}

If the CSA player controls Harper’s Ferry at the end of the turn, it is an automatic CSA victory. If the Jackson HQ is eliminated at any point, it is an automatic USA victory.
