\subsection{WEATHER}

The weather played a very important part in several of the historical campaigns covered in the SHENANDOAH scenarios. When it rains in this part of the country, it really \textit{rains}; the entire countryside turns into a sticky glue and the normally placid rivers turn into raging torrents. The effect on the terrain naturally has a great effect on the ability of armies to move through it. During the Civil War the Valley Turnpike was the only hard-surfaced, all-weather road in the area; all other roads were of dirt, and turned to mud in a downpour.

\begin{enumerate}
  \item The weather for each Turn is determined at the start of each Turn by the Union player rolling one die and consulting the appropriate part of the WEATHER TABLESon the back page of this set of rules.
  \item The die roll determines the weather for that entire Turn. If "Dry" was rolled, the "Dry" portion of the MOVEMENT AND TERRAIN EFFECTS CHART is used; if "Mud" was rolled, the "Mud" portion of the MOVEMENT AND TERRAIN EFFECTS CHART is used.
\end{enumerate}
