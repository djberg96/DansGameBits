\subsubsection{RETREAT MOVEMENT}

\begin{enumerate}
  \item This is handled similarly to withdrawals. A retreat will force the unit or Stack of units back one hex; that hex not being in an enemy Zone of Control if possible. The retreat must be from one hex to one hex, with all units included. Units may be retreated into a hex containing other friendly units and Stacked with them, as long as Stacking Limits are observed.

  \item Units forced to retreat, but which cannot due to be completely encircled by an combination of enemy units in hexes and impassible hexsides (such as ridge hex sides and unfordable river hex sides) are eliminated.

  \item The Phasing Player's and Non-Phasing Player's units that are forced to retreat can do so into any hex, regardless of the number of Movement Allowance Factors that would be expended.

  \item Any of the Phasing Player's units that are forced to retreat are finished with their movement, even if they still have Movement Allowance Factors remaining after their retreat; they must retreat if forced to, even if not enough Movement Allowance FActors are left to make the move in the normal manner.
\end{enumerate}
