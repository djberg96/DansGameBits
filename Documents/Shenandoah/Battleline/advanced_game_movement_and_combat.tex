\subsection{ADVANCED GAME MOVEMENT \& COMBAT}

This section contains rules to make the movement and Combat capabilities of the units more realistic. They are supplementary to the Basic Game rules, none of which are changed, except where noted.

\begin{enumerate}
  \item During the second or subsequent Rounds of Combat, the Phasing Player, during his segment, may exercise this modification of option c) (see Combat Procedure in the Basic Game): "He can remain where he is, and not participate in Combat durin his segment of the Round. If he wishes, he may expend Movement Allowance FActors to change his Formation (i.e. from Column to Battle Formation)." This cannot be done by the Non-Phasing Player.

  \item The requirement for the Phasing Player to attack during the first Round of Combat is dropped. Instead, he may exercise option c) (see Combat Procedure in the Basic Game), although this still uses up Movement Allowance Factors for Combat. This rule is especially useful for units that begin their Phase in an Enemy Zone of Control and do not really wish to attack to disengage, and for Union units that discovered that they have blundered into the Zone of Control of a larger Confederate force (it makes cavalry recon misssions possible). The Phasing player is allowed to move units in Battle Formation into the Zone of Control of enemy units which are across stream of river hexside, as long as he does not attack with infantry or cavalry units.
\end{enumerate}
