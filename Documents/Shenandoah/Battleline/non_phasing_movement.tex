\subsubsection{\mbox{\small NON-PHASING PLAYER MOVEMENT}}

\begin{enumerate}
  \item Normally, the Non-Phasing Player cannot move his units, but must sit and watch the Phasing Player move his. However, the Non-Phasing Player is allowed to move his units that are to be withdrawn \textit{one} hex.

  \item The Non-Phasing Player must be in Column Formation in order to withdraw a hex. He is allowed to change his Formation from Battle Formation to Column Formation in order to withdraw.

  \item A withdrawal is not allowed into a hex that is in the Zone of Control of an enemy unit whose Zone of Control it is already in. However, if no other alternative exists a unit may withdraw into the Zone of Control of an enemy unit to which it was not previously adjacent.

  \item A Stack cannot Unstack and withdraw into two or more different hexes. All withdrawals must be from one hex into another hex with all units included.

  \item A withdrawal may be made into any hex (subject to the above limitations, and not over Prohibited hex sides), without regard to how many Movement Allowance Factors would be expended.

  \item Units may withdraw into a hex containing other friendly units and be Stacked with them, as long as the Stacking Limits are observed.
\end{enumerate}
